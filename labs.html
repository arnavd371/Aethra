aethra labs
students building
tools they wish existed.

labs is where ideas that survive a hackathon keep going. students build AI tools, climate tools, education software, and scientific infrastructure in the open, with documentation that makes the work learnable by others. projects are real, documented, and linked to the people who built them. we do not list projects that are not real.

what we build
◈
artificial intelligence
tools for reasoning, language, alignment research support, and AI that helps students learn harder things faster.
◉
climate and environment
data tools, monitoring systems, and analytical software built around environmental datasets students can actually access.
✦
education technology
tools that make hard subjects more legible. proof assistants, interactive explainers, curriculum gap-fillers.
▶
scientific software
open-source libraries, research pipelines, data visualizations, and computational tools for scientific questions.
how projects get greenlit
01
emerge from a hackathon or fellowship
most labs projects start at an aethra event. a team builds something in 48 hours, it works well enough to be worth continuing, and they want to keep going. that continuation is labs.
02
or apply directly
if you have a project in one of the four tracks that is not connected to an aethra event, email us at aethra.club@gmail.com with a short description of what you're building and why. we will respond within two weeks.
03
build in the open
labs projects are documented publicly. code is open-source where possible. the work is linked to the builders by name. the principle is that doing real work in public is itself valuable for students, independent of outcomes.
04
connect to research if the work is deep enough
some labs projects surface research questions worth pursuing formally. when that happens, aethra helps connect the team to the fellowship track or to a mentor who can help them go deeper.
upcoming initiatives

labs is expanding beyond hackathon follow-ons. these are aethra-run programs that put students in contact with real mathematical and scientific work, outside of competitions.

aethra labs · education technology
proof writing
initiative
in progress

aethra's first proof-writing program: introducing rigorous mathematical proof writing to 4,000 students across grades 3–12 at a partner school. proof writing is largely absent from the Indian curriculum. most students encounter it for the first time in university, without the foundations to make sense of it.

volunteer mathematicians and professors have confirmed to lead workshops. the program is free for every student.

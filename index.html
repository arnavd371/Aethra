the gap
curiosity is
the only
prerequisite.

most students are taught to solve known problems. the curriculum is a map of already-explored territory. the most interesting questions are almost never assigned.

real research is inaccessible to most talented students, not because they lack ability, but because the infrastructure to support them does not exist at their age. no mentors. no communities of peers who care about the same questions. no pathways from curiosity to contribution.

this gap is sharpest in mathematical reasoning. a narrow pipeline of olympiad students encounter proofs and theorem construction before university. everyone else hits formal mathematics for the first time in a first-year lecture, often without the foundations to make sense of it. that is not a talent gap. it is an infrastructure gap.

manifesto
curiosity is
the only
prerequisite.

most students are taught to solve known problems. the curriculum is a map of already-explored territory. the interesting questions — the ones worth spending a life on — are almost never assigned.

research, real research, remains inaccessible to most talented students. not because they lack ability, but because the infrastructure to support them doesn't exist at their age. no mentors. no labs. no communities of peers who care about the same questions.

aethra exists to build that infrastructure. fellowships, open problems, proof workshops, publication support, and a global community of students who take ideas seriously. geography is not destiny. curiosity is enough to start.

we are students building the institution we wish had existed when we were asking our first real questions. we are just getting started.

// red flags to watch for

a growing number of programs target ambitious students with pay-to-prestige schemes — fake journals, paid "research" positions, and conferences that charge hundreds of dollars for a certificate. here's what to look for. practices like these have been documented by outlets including ProPublica.

▶ acceptance in under 48 hours with no stated review process. legitimate competitive programs take weeks to evaluate applications. instant or near-instant acceptance — especially after you submitted nothing substantial — means they're not reviewing anything.
▶ payment required after acceptance, before any work is reviewed. application fees can be legitimate (though worth scrutinising). but being asked to pay only after you're "accepted" — often with a tight deadline to secure your spot — is a standard predatory pattern.
▶ vague or unverifiable judging / editorial board. if you can't independently find any of the named judges or editors online — no university profiles, no published work, no verifiable affiliation — the board likely doesn't exist in the way described.
▶ "guaranteed publication" or "guaranteed certificate of excellence." real journals and awards don't guarantee outcomes before seeing your work. this language exists to sell something, not recognise something.
▶ the same organisation runs the competition and the journal that publishes winners. without independent oversight, there's no separation between the program's financial interests and the selection process. this is a structural conflict of interest, not a credential.
▶ artificial urgency on expensive decisions. "only 3 spots left" or "registration closes in 24 hours" on a program that accepts hundreds of students is a pressure tactic. real selective programs don't need to rush you.
▶ paid mentorship from professors you can't independently verify. if a program charges for "one-on-one mentorship from Ivy League faculty" but the faculty members aren't listed with verifiable contact details or university pages, treat it as unverified until proven otherwise.

research transparency
science
done right.
  • replication studies — reproducing published findings as a research skill, not a punishment
  • failed experiments — documenting what didn't work and why, because that is also research
  • benchmarking — rigorous comparison of methods without cherry-picking results
  • research logs — the habit of writing down what you did, what you assumed, and what surprised you
  • science is only as trustworthy as its practices. we teach the practices, not just the results.

about
the institution
we wish
had existed.

aethra is a global research and innovation ecosystem for students ages 13 to 18, connecting young researchers, builders, and mentors across 10 countries. we are building the intellectual infrastructure that ambitious students wish had existed when they were asking their first real questions.

we started with hackathons — seven events, 1,764 students, judges and sponsors from Google, Apple, Tesla, and IEEE. that foundation taught us what students actually need: not just a stage, but a path from curiosity to contribution. research mentorship, open problems, publication support, proof workshops, and an honest map of the opportunities worth pursuing.

everything aethra offers is free. we have no paid tier, no sponsored rankings, and nothing to sell. that's not a policy — it's the whole point. trustworthy infrastructure can't charge for access to itself.

we believe original thinking is the most important skill a student can develop, and that the conditions for it — good mentors, hard problems, and a community of peers — should not depend on where you were born.
